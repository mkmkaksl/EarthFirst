CURRENT CHALLENGES
Pollution
Every day, humans generate waste that frequently ends up in water bodies, posing a threat to marine ecosystems. In the United States, an annual production of approximately 268 million tons of trash occurs, with roughly 10% of this waste making its way into rivers, oceans, and other aquatic environments. This substantial amount of waste is highly detrimental to marine life, leading to the death of over 1 million marine animals each year.
Fossil Fuels
Wood serves as a crucial resource for constructing various essentials such as houses, paper, and other items. Trees, in general, play a vital role in the environment by absorbing carbon dioxide and releasing fresh oxygen, which is essential for human respiration. Unfortunately, the demand for additional land to accommodate urban development and infrastructure has led to widespread deforestation, resulting in the clearance of trees. Approximately 15 billion trees are felled annually, and this extensive deforestation diminishes the number of trees available to generate oxygen, ultimately posing a threat to human well-being.
Deforestation
The everyday appliances we encounter and utilize in our lives are comprised of numerous minerals. Take, for instance, the lights we use at home, which are constructed with minerals like copper, aluminum, nickel, and others. However, the extraction of these minerals involves mining the Earth, a process that significantly contributes to environmental pollution. Mining activities can lead to adverse effects such as erosion, the formation of sinkholes, biodiversity loss, and contamination of soil, groundwater, and the surrounding regions near the mining site. Remarkably, mining accounts for approximately 7 percent of global greenhouse gas emissions, underscoring its substantial environmental impact.
Mining
The cars that we use, the lights that we turn on, the heater to keep us warm, and many other things all have one thing in common, fossil fuels. These fossil fuels can generate a lot of energy, but at the same time, they generate a lot of pollution. Currently, 89% of global CO2 emissions come from fossil fuels, making it extremely damaging to the environment. These greenhouse gasses cause global warming, which in the end affects us humans, making the use of fossil fuels deadly to everything.
