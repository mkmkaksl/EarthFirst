CURRENT CHALLENGES
Water Pollution
We as humans produce waste everyday, and this waste often finds its way into bodies of water, affecting the marine ecosystems. The U.S alone produces around 268 million tons of trash annually, and around 10% of that find its way into rivers, oceans, and other bodies of water.
All this waste is extremely toxic to marine life, killing over 1 million marine animals each year.
Fossil Fuels
The cars that we use, the lights that we turn on, the heater to keep us warm, and many other things all have one thing in common, fossil fuels. These fossil fuels can generate a lot of energy, but at the same time, they generate a lot of pollution. Currently, 89% of global CO2 emissions come from fossil fuels, making it extremely damaging to the environment. The greenhouse gases from burning fossil fuels cause global warming, which in the end affects us humans, making the use of fossil fuels deadly to everything and everyone.
Deforestation
Wood is an essential resource to building many necessities like houses, paper, and other things. Trees in general are essential to the environment as they take in carbon dioxide and produce fresh oxygen which we humans breathe. However, due to the need for more land to build cities and infrastructure, trees are being cut down to make room. Around 15 billion trees are cut down each year, and all these trees that are cut down are less trees to produce oxygen, thus harming humans in the end.
Mining
The daily appliances that we see and use in our daily lives is made up of lots of minerals. For example, the lights that we use at home are made up of copper, aluminum, nickel, and a few other minerals. However, all these minerals come from mining the Earth, which is a major source of pollution that damages the environment. Mining can cause erosion, sinkholes, loss of biodiversity, and can contaminate the soil, groundwater, and the surrounding areas of the mining site. Mining is responsible for about 7 percent of greenhouse - gas emissions globally.

Tips to be Eco-Smart

A Power Strip

Ever since COVID, more people are starting to work from home so it would be beneficial to install a power strip. When you aren’t using your tech for work, it is often left on - wasting energy. Plugging devices into power strip that can allow you to turn everything off/on at once is convenient & saves energy!

Aerating Faucets And Showerheads

Aerating Faucets & Showerheads are made of screen layers that slow the flow of water, providing more coverage with lower water usage. This is a quick, cost-effective way to save water & money!

CFLs & LEDs

CFLs (Compact Fluorescent Lamps) & LEDs (Light Emmiting Diodes) are very energy efficient. They use 60-80% less energy compared to traditional incandescent bulbs while also lasting longer (saving money in the long run). These bulbs can cost as little as $5 and can save you more than $100!!

Geothermal Heat Pumps

Geothermal heat pumps transfer heat into and out of the home using the ground as both a heat source and a heat sink. They are long lasting (up to 10-15 years) and very durable.

Energy Efficient Windows

Energy efficient windows and insulated siding can keep your house comfortable no matter the weather/season outside. They do this by absorbing the heat from the sun in the winter and reflecting it in the summer.

Solar Panels

Solar Panels, made up of PV (Photovoltaic) cells, convert sunlight into electricity and can outputs anywhere from 10 - 400 watts. Even though the initial investment costs can be as high as $14,696, they can help you save more than $100 per month, which can end up being really helpful in the long-term.
