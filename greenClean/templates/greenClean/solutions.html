Electric Vehicle (EV) Charging Stations
  • Can promote the use of EV's (which are better for the environment!)
  • Fun Fact: All EV's produce 0 direct emissions!
  • Incentives: Sustainable & Quiet!
  • Costs: Expensive & Requires much more time
  • Tax Rebates: Some regions might offer tax credits for installing EV charging stations
    • Community Solar Programs
  • Homeowners can invest in or subscribe to a shared solar energy system!
  • Fun Fact: This system gives people access to clean & renewable energy while giving people credits on their energy bills
  • Incentives: Save money on electrical bills & No need for own solar system
  • Costs: Access is dependent on where you live
  • Tax Rebates: Under the Federal Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) - homeowners can claim a credit that matches 30% of the overall costs they have incurred for solar equipment (includes materials, labor, and installation)
    • Energy-Efficient Lighting
  • LED and CFL bulbs use 60-80% less energy compared to traditional incandescent bulbs while also lasting long!
  • Fun Fact: They can help you save more than $100
  • Incentives: Long term financial savings
  • Costs: High initial costs
  • Tax Rebates: The Federal Inflation Reduction Act passed in 2022 includes over $370 billion in initiatives to speed up the transition to clean energy and lower emissions. This includes energy-efficient appliances.
    • Solar Ventilation
  • Can use sunlight to power fans or vents that improve air circulation
  • Fun Fact: You don't need electricians to help you with Solar Ventilation systems!
  • Incentives: Safer than attic ventilation fans + renewable energy + enhances your homes energy efficiency
  • Costs: High upfront costs
  • Tax Rebates: New Hampshire offers up to $10,000 as rebate for the use of renewable energy
    • Home Energy Monitoring Systems
  • Tracks your home's energy usage in real-time
  • Fun Fact: Can help identify energy-wasting appliances & thus optimize your energy usage!
  • Incentives: Lower long term utility bills
  • Cost: High initial financial cost
  • Tax Rebates: Some regions might offer tax incentives for installing home energy monitoring systems
    • Green Roof Systems
  • Provide insulation, reduce stormwater runoff, and improve air quality
  • Fun Fact: Can reduce energy needs for heating and cooling!
  • Incentives: Increased lifespan of your roof, help the environment & aid air quality
  • Costs: Needs more maintenance & costs more money
  • Tax Rebates: Many regions offer tax rebates for green roof systems. Ex: Florida
